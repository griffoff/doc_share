Don Spear

Founder & CEO, OpenSesame

Meeting: 11:30 – 12:00 PM Goal: Assess acquisition viability.

Personal Background

Don Spear is a seasoned entrepreneur with over 30 years of experience. Prior to co-founding OpenSesame in 2011, he founded BlueVolt.com, a provider of LMS solutions for industrial sectors. His background includes an MBA from Harvard Business School and service as a US Navy submarine officer, indicating a disciplined and strategic mindset.

Company Overview: OpenSesame

OpenSesame operates a large-scale e-learning marketplace for the corporate training and development sector. They position themselves not just as a content provider, but as a comprehensive solution for upskilling and reskilling employees. The company is headquartered in Portland, Oregon, and has seen significant growth, backed by substantial venture capital investment.

Financials & Funding

The company has raised a total of $102M over 6 rounds. Their latest and largest was a $50M Series D round in July 2021, led by JMI Equity. This significant funding underscores investor confidence in their model and provides them with substantial capital for growth.

Marketplace Model

OpenSesame's primary model is a marketplace aggregator. They provide access to over 40,000 courses from a wide range of publishers. This gives customers variety and choice, covering topics from compliance and business skills to highly specific technical training. They operate on a subscription basis, offering curated catalogs to corporate clients.

Value Proposition

Their value extends beyond content aggregation. They focus heavily on curation and personalization, using both human experts ("learning advisors") and AI to create bespoke training plans that align with a company's specific goals and employee skill gaps.

Go-to-Market (GTM) Strategy

Their GTM is B2B, targeting HR and Learning & Development (L&D) departments within mid-market and enterprise companies. A key part of their strategy is seamless integration with existing Learning Management Systems (LMS), which reduces friction for adoption.

Core AI Products

  • Oro: Their patented, AI-powered skills engine. Oro assesses employee skill gaps and automatically delivers tailored learning paths. This is central to their "personalized learning at scale" promise.
  • SmartLists: An AI-powered curation tool that allows L&D managers to input their needs and instantly receive personalized course recommendations.
  • Simon: A course creation tool that uses AI to simplify the development of custom content. It can automatically generate voiceover scripts from slide content.

Strategic Importance of AI

AI is fundamental to their strategy, used to solve the "last mile" problem in corporate learning: connecting the right content to the right learner at the right time. By automating personalization and curation, they increase learner engagement and administrative efficiency. They are also capitalizing on the market trend by offering extensive training *on* AI.

Key Competitors

The corporate e-learning space is crowded. Main competitors include large content libraries (LinkedIn Learning, Udemy Business), LXPs (Degreed), and other aggregators (Go1).

Competitive Differentiation

OpenSesame differentiates itself through:

  1. Quality over Quantity: A rigorous 42-point quality review for all publishers.
  2. Advanced AI-driven Personalization: Investment in tools like Oro to provide relevant learning paths.
  3. Human + AI Curation: A blend of dedicated learning advisors with AI tools for a high-touch service.

Potential for Cengage

Acquisition Target: Acquiring OpenSesame would provide Cengage with an immediate, strong foothold in the corporate L&D market, aligning with a strategy to diversify beyond higher education into workforce development.

Synergistic Opportunities:

  • Content Distribution: Cengage's content could be distributed through the OpenSesame marketplace.
  • Technology Integration: OpenSesame's AI-driven curation and skills-mapping tech could be integrated into Cengage's platforms.

Key Questions & Talking Points

  1. "Don, how do you see AI evolving from a personalization tool to a core part of the content creation and credentialing process in the next 3-5 years?"
  2. "What percentage of your catalog is from third-party publishers versus content generated via your 'Simon' tool? How do you see that mix changing?"
  3. "Given your growth and Series D funding, what does the future look like for OpenSesame? Are you focused on private growth, or exploring strategic partnerships or an exit?"

Terah Crews

CEO, ReUp Education

Meeting: 2:00 – 2:50 PM Goal: Explore partnership potential.

Personal Background

Terah Crews is the CEO of ReUp Education and a recognized leader in postsecondary education and student success. Her career has been focused on creating pathways to opportunity for adult learners. Her presence as a facilitator on "Learner Re-engagement" underscores her expertise and passion in this specific niche.

Company Overview: ReUp Education

Founded in 2015, ReUp is a mission-driven company that focuses exclusively on a massive, underserved population: the 40+ million US adults with some college credit but no degree. They partner directly with colleges, universities, and state systems to re-engage these "stopped-out" students and support them through to graduation.

Technology-Enabled Service Model

ReUp's model is a partnership-based, tech-enabled service. They are not a university or a content provider. Instead, they act as a service layer that sits between an institution and its former students, providing outreach and coaching.

Revenue Model

ReUp typically operates on a revenue-share model. They partner with institutions and share in the tuition revenue generated from the students they successfully re-enroll. This creates a powerful alignment of incentives: ReUp only succeeds when the student and the institution succeed. This model is highly attractive to universities as it requires little to no upfront investment.

Predictive Analytics Core

The foundation of ReUp's success is its sophisticated use of data. They leverage historical student data from their partners and apply patented predictive scoring models. This allows them to segment the stopped-out population and identify individuals with the highest propensity to return and succeed, making their outreach far more efficient.

Personalized Coaching Platform

While data identifies the students, technology also enables the coaching. ReUp's coaches use a platform that provides a holistic view of each student's journey, tracks their progress, and helps manage communication, ensuring personalized support can be delivered at scale.

Proven Success at Scale

ReUp's impact is significant and quantifiable, which is a key part of their value proposition to partners. Their success validates their model and demonstrates a clear ROI for participating institutions.

Recent Recognition

ReUp was recently named to the 2025 Inc. 5000 list of fastest-growing private companies, a testament to the scalability and demand for their solution.

A Highly Synergistic Partnership

A partnership between Cengage and ReUp appears to be an exceptionally strong strategic fit. ReUp solves the "demand" problem for higher education (getting students back in seats), while Cengage provides the "supply" (the learning materials and platforms).

Potential Partnership Models:

  • Referral & Co-Marketing: Cengage could introduce ReUp's services to its vast network of university partners as a value-added solution to boost enrollment.
  • Integrated Solution: A deeper partnership could involve creating an integrated offering, bundling Cengage Unlimited for re-enrolled students.

Key Questions & Talking Points

  1. "Terah, as you bring learners back, what are the biggest challenges they face with course materials and digital learning platforms?"
  2. "We see a powerful synergy between your service and Cengage Unlimited. Have you explored bundling learning materials into your re-engagement packages?"
  3. "At Cengage, we are deeply focused on student success. What would an ideal collaboration look like from your perspective to maximize our combined impact on student completion?"
